# Global Economic & Political Events: May 2025 – March 2026
## Research Compilation (Excludes US and China — covered separately)

**Compiled:** March 12, 2026
**Coverage:** Russia/Ukraine, Europe, Japan, Middle East, Global Markets, Other Major Events

## 1. RUSSIA–UKRAINE WAR

### Peace Negotiations Status
- Trump's administration took the lead on peace talks from early 2025, formulating proposals generally favoring Russia's position; both Russia and Ukraine initially rejected these offers.
- By **December 2025**, US and Ukrainian officials coalesced around a revised **20-point framework** covering ceasefire terms, security guarantees, and territorial disputes.
- **January 2026 (Paris talks):** France and the UK pledged to deploy troops to Ukrainian territory as part of a security guarantee arrangement. The US backed a truce monitoring mechanism.
- European/US/Ukrainian officials described the deal as "**90% complete**" — but Russia continues to refuse, insisting on recognition of territorial claims (full Donbas annexation) and resisting constraints on its military posture.
- Putin repeatedly spurned Zelenskyy's ceasefire offers, including a Christmas 2025 proposal.
- **UN General Assembly** voted on a ceasefire resolution at the 4th anniversary of the invasion (February 2026).

### Russian Economy
- **GDP Growth:** IMF forecasts ~0.6% in 2025, ~1.0% in 2026. Russian Academy of Sciences projects 0.7% in 2025, 1.4% in 2026. The economy is adapting but growing slowly under war conditions and sanctions.
- **Budget Pressures:** Oil & gas revenues dropped >25% due to ruble appreciation and the sanctions regime; import tax receipts also down. Budget deficit pressures growing.
- **2026 Outlook (Moscow Times):** "More war, slower growth, and higher taxes."

### Sanctions Updates
- EU extended economic sanctions for another 6 months in **December 2025**.
- **October 2025:** US and UK sanctioned Rosneft and Lukoil (Russia's two largest oil companies), causing significant oil market volatility.
- **Oil price cap** reduced from $60/bbl to **$47.6/bbl** (July 2025 EU decision).
- **January 2026:** EU Council gave final approval for a **stepwise ban on Russian gas imports**:
  - LNG from short-term contracts: banned from April 25, 2026
  - Pipeline gas from short-term contracts: banned from June 17, 2026
  - LNG long-term contracts: banned from January 1, 2027
  - Pipeline gas long-term contracts: banned by September 30 – November 1, 2027

### Energy Market Impact
- Russian gas still accounted for ~13% of EU imports in 2025, worth >€15B/year (down from ~40% pre-war).
- Russian oil imports to EU dropped to <3% by 2025.
- Ukraine–Russia gas transit contract through Ukraine expired **January 1, 2025**, cutting pipeline flows through Ukraine permanently.
- Germany, Italy, Netherlands added new floating storage regasification units (FSRUs) for LNG.

### Ruble Exchange Rate
- Ruble hovered around **77–79 per USD** in early 2026, near 3-year highs reached in late 2025 (75.5 per USD).
- Supported by: capital controls, Bank of Russia's high interest rates, mandatory export earnings conversion.
- Long-term strength depends on sanctions relief — which is unlikely in 2026.

## 2. EUROPE

### ECB Rate Decisions (May 2025 – March 2026)
- ECB continued a **rate-cutting cycle** that began June 2024, but paused by mid-2025.
- **September 2025:** ECB held rates — deposit facility at 2.00%, main refinancing at 2.15%, marginal lending at 2.40%.
- **December 18, 2025:** ECB held rates **unchanged** for the 4th consecutive meeting.
- **February 5, 2026:** ECB held rates for the **5th consecutive meeting** (2.00% deposit rate).
- ~85% of economists surveyed by Reuters (January 2026) expect ECB to hold through 2026.
- Staff project inflation below 2% on average in 2026 and 2027; next policy move expected to be a rate **hike**, but not until 2027–2028.

### EU Economic Performance
- **EU GDP:** Projected +1.4% in both 2025 and 2026; Eurozone +1.3% in 2025, +1.2% in 2026.
- No recession — all member states expected to grow in 2025 for the first time since 2021.
- Drivers: rising real wages, government spending (NextGen EU + defense), inventory cycle reversal.
- Laggards: Germany and Italy. Leaders: Poland and Spain.
- US-EU trade deal expected to shave ~0.5 percentage points from EU growth.

### Germany — Merz Government
- **February 2025 election** → CDU/CSU-led coalition formed. **Friedrich Merz** became Chancellor in **May 2025**.
- Key policies announced:
  - **€500 billion special infrastructure fund** to bypass debt brake for rail, digital infrastructure
  - Constitutional reform exempting defense spending >1% of GDP from fiscal constraints
  - Corporate tax cuts and streamlined construction permitting
  - "Climate pragmatism" replacing prior moral-based climate policy
- **Economic situation:** Economy contracted in both 2023 and 2024. Growth projected at only 0.1% in 2025, 0.7%–0.8% in 2026.
- Merz warned in January 2026 that Germany is at an economic "crossroads" and needs "radical" steps; current measures have been insufficient.
- Energy: Working on Hydrogen Core Network for steel and chemical decarbonization by 2032.
- Euronews critique: Merz "chiseling economic decline into the German constitution" via fiscal conservatism.

### France — Macron Political Situation
- France experienced an **unprecedented political crisis** following hung parliament from 2024 elections (three-way split: NFP left, Ensemble center, National Rally far-right).
- **Four minority governments** appointed in quick succession: Barnier → Bayrou → Lecornu (resigned after 1 day) → Lecornu reappointed.
- **Sébastien Lecornu** appointed PM September 9, 2025; cabinet unveiled October 5; resigned October 6 — shortest-serving PM in Fifth Republic history — then reappointed.
- Macron's approval rating fell to a **record-low 28%** (down from 61% in 2017).
- Trust in national government dropped 13 points to 29%.
- **Credit rating:** KBRA downgraded France's long-term sovereign rating to AA-, citing deficits and deteriorating debt trajectory.
- Debt-to-GDP: ~116% in 2025, forecast to rise to ~130% by 2030.
- Debt servicing costs surging: €59.3B forecast for 2026 (up from €36.2B in 2020).
- **Military:** French parliament approved Macron's military spending boost in 2026 budget.
- Growth outlook: resilient but constrained; modest recovery expected as inflation eases.

### UK — Starmer Government Economic Performance
- UK GDP grew a feeble **+0.1% in Q4 2025** (half the consensus forecast).
- OBR forecasts: **+1.5%** for full-year 2025 (fastest-growing G7 European economy), then **+1.4% in 2026**.
- CPI inflation: fell to **3.0%** in January 2026 (down from 3.4% in December 2025).
- Unemployment: 1.88M people in Q4 2025, up 331,000 year-on-year — youth unemployment especially rising.
- Government claims UK grew faster than any other European G7 economy in 2025.
- Bloomberg: "A stumbling UK economy is adding to Starmer's woes" (February 2026).

### Brexit Economic Effects
- By 2025, UK economy is estimated to be **~8% smaller** than it would have been without Brexit (macro data); 6% smaller on firm-level data.
- UK's performance increasingly lagging international peers — growth, living standards, economic league table rankings all slipped.
- Brexit non-tariff barriers raised UK–EU trade costs by ~2–12% in tariff-equivalent terms; largest for food products.
- UK worldwide exports reduced ~6.4%, imports ~3.1% from Brexit.
- **May 2025 EU-UK summit** (Lancaster House): symbolic thaw, "reset" in relations — but economic implications assessed as modest.

### European Response to US Strikes on Iran and Venezuela
- France and UK pledged forces to Ukraine security guarantee framework (January 2026) in context of wider geopolitical shifts.
- European nations deeply concerned about Strait of Hormuz disruption and oil price spike following February/March 2026 Iran strikes.
- EU facing energy supply risks with oil above $94/bbl (March 9, 2026) following Iran war onset.

## 3. JAPAN

### BOJ Policy — Rate Hikes
- **July 2025:** BOJ raised rates to 0.50% (25 bp hike).
- **December 2025:** BOJ raised rates again to **0.75%** — highest since 1995; 10-year JGB yield rose past 2%.
- Further hikes expected but not imminent:
  - Most likely timing: **June 2026** (post-spring Shunto wage negotiations)
  - Alternative: October 2026
  - Some analysts expect 2–4 more hikes over next 2 years

### Yen Exchange Rate
- Despite rate hikes, yen remained **soft** throughout late 2025, trading around **156–157 per USD**.
- Strategists at JPMorgan, BNP Paribas forecast yen **weakening to 160/USD or beyond** by end-2026.
- Drivers of weakness: still-wide US-Japan yield gap, negative real rates, persistent capital outflows.
- BOJ unlikely to rush additional hikes unless yen falls below 155 toward 152.

### Japan Leadership Change — Takaichi Succeeds Ishiba
- PM Shigeru Ishiba announced resignation **September 7, 2025** following LDP-Komeito coalition losing majority in House of Councillors.
- LDP presidential election won by **Sanae Takaichi** — Japan's **first female Prime Minister**, elected by parliament **October 21, 2025**.
- Takaichi is ultraconservative with ties to far-right LDP factions; won 237 vs 149 votes in lower house.

### Japan Economic Policy
- Japan recorded a trade deficit of ~$15B (2.2 trillion yen) for H1 2025.
- Q1 2025 GDP contracted ~0.7% annualized due to tariff uncertainty.
- Toyota forecast **35% profit loss**, Honda **70% profit loss** for fiscal year 2026 due to US tariffs.

### Japan–US Trade Relations Under Trump
- April 2025: Trump imposed 10% "reciprocal tariffs" on Japan; threatened 25% country-specific tariff from August 1.
- **July 23, 2025: US-Japan Trade Deal** — settled at **15% tariff rate** (avoiding threatened 25%).
- Japan committed to purchase US aircraft and defense equipment, and to invest **$550 billion** in strategic US sectors.
- USMCA-equivalent deal; framework agreement praised for preventing worst-case tariff scenario.
- CUSS framework under review for post-Ishiba (Takaichi) government continuity.

## 4. MIDDLE EAST (POST-KHAMENEI)

### The Twelve-Day War — June 2025
- **June 2025:** Israel launched airstrikes on Iran targeting nuclear facilities (Natanz, Fordow, Isfahan) and military/regime infrastructure, with US involvement.
- **June 24, 2025:** Ceasefire announced, ending the 12-Day War.
- Key effect: significantly degraded Iran's nuclear program; but did NOT decapitate leadership at this stage.

### The 2026 Iran–Israel–US War
- **February 28, 2026:** US-Israeli forces launched a "decapitation" strike killing Supreme Leader **Ali Khamenei** and several family members in Tehran.
- This represented a dramatic strategic pivot — from nuclear degradation to leadership assassination.
- **March 1, 2026:** Iran's Interim Leadership Council formed (per Article 111 of the Constitution):
  - Alireza Arafi (Guardian Council)
  - Chief Justice Gholam-Hossein Mohseni-Eje'i
  - Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf
  - President Masoud Pezeshkian
- **March 8, 2026:** Iran's Assembly of Experts announced consensus on successor.
- **March 11, 2026:** **Mojtaba Khamenei** (Khamenei's son, age 56) named Iran's **third Supreme Leader**.
  - Hard-line; close ties to Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC).
  - Named by the Assembly of Experts.
- Iran's military restricted Strait of Hormuz traffic — Brent crude spiked to **$94/bbl** by March 9, 2026.
- Iran launched strikes across 9 countries: Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Oman, UAE, and others.
- Lebanon: Hezbollah resumed strikes on Israel on **March 2, 2026** (Lebanon ceasefire effectively broke down).

### Oil Prices Trajectory
- Long-term baseline (pre-Iran war): EIA forecast Brent falling from avg $69/bbl in 2025 to **$58/bbl** in 2026, $53/bbl in 2027 — due to supply glut.
- J.P. Morgan forecast Brent avg ~**$60/bbl** in 2026 under normal conditions.
- **OPEC+ agreed March 1, 2026** to begin raising production +206,000 b/d from April 2026.
- **War premium:** Brent surged from $71/bbl (Feb 27) to **$94/bbl** (March 9, 2026) following Iran war onset and Hormuz restrictions.
- Global energy crisis fears; likely sustained elevated prices while Hormuz disruption continues.

### Saudi Arabia Economic Diversification
- Non-oil GDP now **52% of total Saudi GDP** (up from 47% in 2016 baseline).
- Non-oil government revenues: record **SR505.3 billion** in 2025.
- ICT sector: estimated at **15.6% of GDP**.
- Non-oil exports grew from $47.4B (baseline) to **~$82B** (2025).
- **IMF projections:** GDP +3.5% in 2025, +3.9% in 2026.
- **Budget deficit** forecast ~4% of GDP in 2026 (wider than expected, due to lower oil prices).
- Tourism target: 10% of GDP and 1.6M jobs by 2030.
- Saudi Arabia's infrastructure was hit by Iranian strikes during the March 2026 war (civilian infrastructure in Gulf states targeted).

### Israel–Gaza–Lebanon Situation
- **January 19 – March 18, 2025:** First ceasefire between Israel and Hamas-led groups.
- **March 18, 2025:** Israel relaunched surprise airstrikes on Gaza, breaking the ceasefire.
- **October 10, 2025:** Third ceasefire took effect (US-backed, phase one of peace plan).
- **January 14, 2026:** Phase two announced by US Special Envoy Steve Witkoff. Broadly holding with sporadic IDF strikes killing 442+ Palestinians as of mid-January 2026.
- **Lebanon:** November 2024 ceasefire (Israel–Hezbollah) violated >10,000 times by Israel per UNIFIL.
- **March 2, 2026:** Lebanon ceasefire broke down; Hezbollah resumed full strikes on Israel following Khamenei assassination.
- Region now in open-ended multi-front war with no diplomatic off-ramps.

## 5. GLOBAL MARKETS

### Major Market Indices Performance (2025)
- Global equities remained broadly resilient in 2025 despite tariff-driven trade shocks.
- **Sharp drawdown:** ~18% from February 18 peak to April 7 trough (triggered by Trump's "reciprocal tariff" announcements).
- Strong recovery thereafter.
- **S&P 500:** +16% for 2025 (second-lowest among major markets).
- **Nikkei 225:** +61.9% from April 7 – December 25, 2025 — one of best-performing markets globally.
- **DAX (Germany):** +3.7% year-to-date (modest; Germany's economic stagnation a drag).
- 2026 outlook: Dow Jones, Nikkei 225, Hang Seng expected to outperform. Capital continuing to rotate from expensive US mega-cap tech into Europe and Asia.

### Commodity Prices

#### Oil
- See Middle East section above. Brent: baseline ~$58–60/bbl for 2026, war premium pushed to $94/bbl March 2026.

#### Gold
- Gold surged **>40% in 2025** — historically large investment-driven rally.
- Driven by: trade tensions, central bank purchases, ETF demand, geopolitical uncertainty.
- J.P. Morgan forecast: avg **$5,055/oz** by Q4 2026, rising toward **$5,400/oz** end-2027.
- Precious metals projected to rise a further **5% in 2026** after 2025's record gains.

#### Copper
- Copper prices surged **>20%** since start of 2025, driven by supply shortage and AI-related demand.
- J.P. Morgan forecast: avg **~$12,075/mt** for full-year 2026 (peak ~$12,500/mt in Q2 2026).
- Goldman Sachs more cautious: fair value ~$11,500/t, forecast $11,200/t in Q4 2026.
- AI infrastructure buildout and energy transition are structural demand drivers.

### Cryptocurrency — Bitcoin
- Bitcoin reached an **all-time high of ~$123,339** in August 2025.
- Ended 2025 around **~$99,887** — "bruised but structurally strong."
- US national Bitcoin strategic reserve: blueprint pending legislative action as of early 2026.
- Corporate Bitcoin holdings: ~672,497 BTC collectively across major corporate actors.
- One major stablecoin issuer acquired 8,888 BTC in recent quarter.
- 2026 analyst forecasts vary widely: $58K–$230K range; structural bullish thesis from institutional adoption, ETF inflows, regulatory clarity.
- Q1 2026 seen as consolidation phase.

### Major Financial Institution Failures
- **2025:** Only 2 small US banks failed (Pulaski Savings Bank, Chicago; Santa Anna National Bank, Texas) — both tiny (largest: $63.8M assets). Plus 5 small credit unions.
- **2026 first failure:** Metropolitan Capital Bank & Trust (Chicago, $261M assets) — failed early 2026, sold to First Independence Bank, FDIC cost ~$19.7M.
- No systemic failures. However:
  - 73% of banking risk professionals expect moderate-to-significant system instability over next 3–5 years.
  - 1,788 US banks have CRE exposure >300% of equity capital — maturity wall peaks 2025–2027.
  - Commercial real estate remains the primary systemic risk.

## 6. OTHER MAJOR EVENTS

### Key Elections with Economic Significance

#### Canada
- **April 28, 2025:** Liberal Party under **Mark Carney** won federal election (minority government).
- Carney campaigned on standing up to Trump's tariffs and "protecting Canada's economy."
- Trump tariffs on Canada: 25% on non-USMCA goods, 10% on energy, 25% on steel/aluminum, 25% on autos. Later threatened to raise to 35%.
- Carney declared "the old relationship between Canada and the US is over."
- USMCA comes up for renegotiation in **July 2026** — major upcoming flashpoint.
- Canadian government counter-measures: retaliatory tariffs, "Buy Canadian" campaigns, export diversification.

#### Australia
- **May 3, 2025:** Labor under PM **Anthony Albanese** won federal election strongly.
- Economic priorities: productivity, skills, data/digital, care economy, clean energy transition.
- Albanese maintained since May 2022.

#### Germany
- February 2025 election → Merz coalition (see Europe section).

#### 2026 Elections to Watch
- Multiple elections globally with economic significance flagged for 2026 (CFR "Ten Elections to Watch").

### COP30 — Climate and Energy Policy
- **COP30 held in Belém, Brazil, November 2025.**
- Outcomes:
  - First-ever **Just Transition mechanism** for workers, Indigenous peoples, frontline communities.
  - Reaffirmed tripling renewable energy capacity globally by 2030.
  - Climate finance boost — aiming to mobilize **$1.3 trillion/year by 2035**.
  - Public utility pledges: $66B/year for renewables, $82B for transmission/storage.
- **Key failure:** Final text did NOT include a roadmap to fund/operationalize the COP28 "transition away from fossil fuels" commitment, despite >80 nations pushing for it.
- Global renewable energy investment reached a record **$2.4 trillion in 2024**, but growth slowing (7.3% vs 32% prior year). 90% of investment concentrated in advanced economies + China.

### AI Industry — Economic Impact
- **Massive investment:** Top 5 US tech companies expected to spend up to **$700B on AI infrastructure** in 2026.
- AI-related capital expenditures contributed **~1.1% to US GDP** growth in H1 2025.
- Harvard economist Jason Furman: AI investments accounted for 92% of US GDP growth H1 2025 — without it, growth would be near 0.1%.
- Goldman Sachs chief economist Jan Hatzius counterpoint: actual GDP impact from AI was "basically zero" in 2025 (investment not yet generating output-level productivity).
- AI goods trade drove WTO-documented spike in global merchandise trade H1 2025.
- Long-term: Penn Wharton estimates AI increases productivity/GDP by 1.5% by 2035, ~3% by 2055.
- Labor market: accelerating efficiency but no large-scale employment collapse yet. Data centers creating high-skill, high-wage jobs.

### Global Trade Volume Trends
- **WTO revised 2025 global trade growth UP to 2.4%** (from earlier lower estimates).
- H1 2025: +4.9% year-on-year (5.5% Q1, 4.3% Q2) — driven by AI goods spending and US import frontloading ahead of tariffs.
- **2026 outlook: sharply downgraded to just 0.5% growth** (down from 1.8% prior estimate).
- Cause of slowdown: full-year tariff impact, global economic cooling.
- Services trade growth also slowing: 6.8% in 2024 → 4.6% in 2025 → 4.4% in 2026.
- Major downside risk: spread of trade-restrictive measures beyond US to more economies/sectors.
