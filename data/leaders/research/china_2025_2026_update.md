## 1. MILITARY PURGE

### Zhang Youxia — Arrest Details

- **Date of announcement:** January 24, 2026. China's Ministry of National Defense announced Zhang Youxia and Liu Zhenli had been placed under investigation for "serious violations of discipline and law."
- **Position held:** CMC Vice Chairman (second-in-command of the entire PLA; the most senior military figure below Xi himself).
- **Background:** Zhang Youxia was one of Xi's oldest and closest political allies — they are "princelings" of the same revolutionary generation. His arrest is therefore historically unprecedented.
- **Interpretation:** Most analysts believe the arrest stems from Xi's investigation into PLA corruption following lessons drawn from Russia's poor performance in Ukraine (2022 onward). Xi reportedly discovered rampant procurement corruption and graft tied to promotions across the Rocket Force and beyond. The arrest may also reflect professional disagreement: the timing — January 2026, entering the final training cycle before Xi's declared 2027 Taiwan readiness deadline — suggests Zhang may have pushed back on Xi's accelerated joint-operations timeline.
- **One fringe but noted hypothesis:** Zhang's removal is described by some outlets as a "coup attempt" that Xi preempted; the PLA was put on "highest alert" around January 24–29.

### Liu Zhenli — Arrest Details

- **Position held:** Chief of the CMC Joint Staff Department (equivalent to Chairman of the Joint Chiefs in the US). Oversaw all C4ISR capabilities.
- **Arrested simultaneously with Zhang Youxia** on January 24, 2026.
- **Significance:** The Joint Staff Department position is now vacant for the first time since the Cultural Revolution. This means the PLA's operational planning and command-and-control apparatus has no confirmed head.

### Total Generals Purged Since 2022

| Category | Count |
|---|---|
| Officially purged generals/lieutenant generals since 2022 | 36 |
| Total senior PLA leaders purged (CSIS count, including post-retirement purges) | **101** |
| Officially purged in 2025 alone (9 expelled from CCP + 6 dismissed) | 15 |
| General officers who "went missing" in 2025 (absent from key events) | ~46 |
| CMC-level purges (most senior body) | 6 |

**CMC-level purges include:** Wei Fenghe (former Defense Minister), Li Shangfu (former Defense Minister), He Weidong (CMC Vice Chairman), Miao Hua (Political Work Department Director), Liu Zhenli (Joint Staff Chief), and Zhang Youxia (CMC Senior Vice Chairman).

When accounting for positions purged more than once, **52% of the PLA's 176 top leadership positions** have been affected since 2022.

### Current PLA Command Structure (as of March 2026)

- **CMC Chairman:** Xi Jinping (sole effective decision-maker)
- **CMC Vice Chairman:** Zhang Shengmin (career political commissar; no operational command experience; elevated at the Fourth Plenum, October 2025)
- **Joint Staff Department Chief:** Vacant
- **Status:** The CMC is now reduced to a two-person body (Xi + Zhang Shengmin), rendering it "a collective defense decision-making organ in name only." This is described as unprecedented since the Cultural Revolution.
- **Cascading effects:** The purge vortex is now rippling into frontline units. Units opposite Taiwan (notably the 73rd Group Army) have been heavily affected — He Weidong, Miao Hua, and former Eastern Theater Command Commander Lin Xiangyang all came from that formation.
- **February 26, 2026:** A further purge wave was announced (The Diplomat: "China's February 26 Military Purge: The Domino Effect"), continuing the cascade.
- **Defense budget (2026):** Proposed 7% increase to ~1.91 trillion yuan (~$277 billion USD), approved at the 2026 Two Sessions.

### Connection to Taiwan Planning

- Xi declared a 2027 deadline for PLA readiness to invade Taiwan; the purges are occurring in the final training cycles before that window.
- Analysts are split: some argue the purges fatally degrade readiness; others argue Xi is ensuring personal loyalty over competence before any operation.
- The "Justice Mission 2025" exercise (December 29–30, 2025) specifically rehearsed a Taiwan blockade with 130 aircraft sorties, 90 of which crossed the Taiwan Strait centerline.
- CSIS February 2026 report: "Scale of Xi's military purges could hinder China's ability to fight."

## 2. ECONOMY

### GDP Growth 2025 — Actual Numbers

- **Full-year 2025 GDP growth: 5.0%** (hit official target exactly)
- **Total GDP:** 140.19 trillion yuan (surpassed 140 trillion yuan threshold for first time; ~$20.4 trillion USD at average exchange rate)
- **Per capita GDP:** 99,665 yuan (~$13,953 USD)
- **Quarterly deceleration:**
  - Q1 2025: +5.4% YoY
  - Q2 2025: +5.2% YoY
  - Q3 2025: +4.8% YoY
  - Q4 2025: +4.5% YoY
- **2026 target:** 4.5%–5% (announced at March 2026 Two Sessions)
- **Net exports contributed 1.6 pp to growth** — trade front-loading ahead of US tariffs was a key factor in early 2025 strength.

### Deflation / Inflation Situation

- **2025 full-year CPI: 0.0%** — technically avoided deflation but missed the official ~2% target badly
- **2025 full-year PPI: -2.6%** — factory-gate deflation now entering its **fourth consecutive year**
- **December 2025 CPI:** Rose to a 34-month high on stimulus/holiday effects
- **January 2026 CPI:** +0.2% YoY (weak)
- **February 2026 CPI:** +1.3% YoY (3-year high; largely Lunar New Year effect; beat market expectation of 0.8%)
- **February 2026 PPI:** -0.9% YoY (slowest deflation pace in over a year; better than expected -1.2%)
- **Assessment:** Structural demand weakness persists. Consumer prices remain well below target. Producer deflation, though easing, reflects overcapacity in industry. Deflationary psychology is embedded.

### Property Market

**Evergrande:**
- Delisted from Hong Kong Stock Exchange in August 2025 after trading remained suspended >18 months following January 2024 court-ordered liquidation.
- Left scars across the sector but is no longer an active crisis node.

**Vanke — The New Crisis Node (2025–2026):**
- State-backed developer China Vanke (once China's largest homebuilder) came close to default in late December 2025.
- Narrowly avoided defaulting on a 2 billion yuan bond (December 2025); bondholders agreed to extend deadlines.
- **Cash position (September 2025):** 60 billion yuan vs. short-term debts of 151 billion yuan.
- Shenzhen Metro Group (state-controlled) injected >29 billion yuan in shareholder loans throughout 2025.
- **Projected 2025 net loss: 82 billion yuan (~$11.8 billion USD)**, widening from 49.5 billion yuan in 2024.
- Still negotiating debt extensions for January 2026 obligations.

**Sector-Wide:**
- Real estate investment in first 10 months of 2025: **-14.7%**
- New home sales (full year 2025): **-8%** (fifth consecutive year of negative growth)
- New home sales by value (Jan–Nov 2025): **-11.2%** YoY
- Home prices: down **20%+ from 2021 peak**
- Market has not found bottom; searching for floor as of early 2026.

**Policy Response:**
- Shanghai and Beijing relaxed suburban home-buying rules; purchase caps removed, mortgage rates slashed.
- Beijing's December 2025 CEWC outlined stabilizing real estate as a **primary 2026 focus**: controlling new supply, reducing existing inventory, optimizing supply structure.
- Beijing directing local governments to accelerate lending to developers; considering plan for state-owned companies to absorb unsold homes from distressed developers.
- The policy model has shifted to **"selective stabilization rather than broad rescues"**.

### Stock Market Performance 2025

| Index | 2025 Full-Year Return |
|---|---|
| Hang Seng (Hong Kong) | **+28%** (best year since 2017) |
| CSI 300 (mainland) | **+18%** (best since 2020) |
| Shanghai Composite | Hit a decade high in September 2025 |

- Key drivers: May 2025 US-China tariff truce, DeepSeek AI enthusiasm, government stimulus flows, foreign investor re-engagement.
- Both Hong Kong and mainland stocks posted **second consecutive year of gains** in 2025.
- Volatility episodes: November 2025 tech rout (Vanke debt jitters), recurring trade-war uncertainty.

### Youth Unemployment 2025

- **Peak 2025:** 18.9% in August 2025 (record high; coincided with 12.22 million new graduates entering workforce, also a record)
- **June 2025:** 14.5% (12-month low at time; fourth consecutive monthly decline)
- **November 2025:** 16.9%
- **December 2025:** 16.5% (lowest since June 2025)
- **Total urban jobless youth (~20 million):** roughly 20 million Gen Z city-dwellers unemployed
- Job postings for graduates dropped 22% in H1 2025; job seekers rose 8%.
- Metric note: all figures exclude students (revised methodology adopted 2023).

### Stimulus Packages 2025

**Fiscal:**
- Budget deficit raised to **4% of GDP** — highest in 30+ years.
- 1.3 trillion yuan ($178.9 billion) in ultra-long-term special treasury bonds; 300 billion yuan more than 2024.
- The Private Economy Promotion Law passed — signals intent to revitalize private-sector investment.
- Stimulus was **infrastructure-heavy, not consumption-driven** — structural rebalancing was not addressed.

**Monetary (shift announced late 2024, implemented 2025):**
- Policy stance officially changed from "prudent" (held 2010–2024) to **"moderately loose"** — a significant formal shift.
- Reserve requirement ratio cut: **-50 basis points**
- 7-day reverse repo rate cut: **-20 basis points** (1.7% → 1.5%)
- 1-year MLF rate cut: **-30 basis points**
- Assessment: Monetary easing primarily facilitated government spending; did not materially boost household or corporate credit demand.

**IMF 2025 Article IV (February 2026):** Confirmed growth but flagged risks: structural demand weakness, property overhang, deflation persistence.

### RMB/USD Exchange Rate

- **2025 average:** 1 USD = **7.1874 CNY**
- **2025 range:** 6.9955 (strongest) to 7.3499 (weakest)
- **USD/CNY moved -4.14% in 2025** (yuan strengthened vs. dollar)
- **Early 2026:** Rate trending toward 6.85–6.95 range; Fed Reserve data March 2, 2026 shows further yuan appreciation vs. 2025 average.

## 3. TRADE WAR RESPONSE

### Chronology: US-China Tariff War 2025

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| Early 2025 | Trump 2.0 tariff escalation begins against China |
| March 10, 2025 | China retaliates: 15% on US chicken, wheat, corn, cotton; 10% on soybeans, pork, beef, dairy, etc. |
| April 4, 2025 | China introduces rare earth export controls (first wave, 7 elements) as retaliation for further Trump tariffs |
| May 12, 2025 | **US-China 90-day tariff truce** announced — bilateral tariff rates reduced; markets rally |
| September 2025 | Chinese stocks declared "on fire"; tariff truce cited as key driver |
| October 9–10, 2025 | China expands rare earth controls (second wave; 5 more elements; extraterritorial provisions) |
| October 12, 2025 | Trump threatens 100% tariffs on China; China responds: "We do not want a tariff war but we are not afraid of one" |
| November 2025 | **Extended truce agreement**: one-year extension of tariff reductions through November 10, 2026 |
| Late 2025 | CNBC analysis: "China won the 2025 battle in Trump's trade war" |
| January 16, 2026 | Canada-China bilateral deal: Chinese EVs at 6.1% tariff (vs. earlier 100%+); China lowers canola tariff to ~15% |

### China's Trade Diversification Strategy

- Rerouted export flows through Southeast Asia and Mexico to blunt US tariff impact.
- Reduced US oil imports by ~90%; shifted to Canada.
- Stopped buying US soybeans; shifted to Argentina, Uruguay, Brazil.
- Pursuing ~20 new trade agreements with EU, Gulf states, Canada, ASEAN, trans-Pacific blocs.
- ASEAN upgraded FTA came into effect October 2025 (expanded digital economy, e-commerce, green trade).
- China is actively seeking CPTPP membership.

### Rare Earth Export Controls (Full Detail)

**Wave 1 — April 4, 2025:**
- MOFCOM Notice 2025 No. 61: export controls on 7 rare earth elements.
- Immediate response to Trump's latest tariff round.

**Wave 2 — October 9, 2025 (effective November 8, 2025):**
- 5 additional rare earth elements: holmium, erbium, thulium, europium, ytterbium.
- **Extraterritorial provisions:** Export licenses required for products made *outside* China if they contain Chinese-origin materials at ≥0.1% value threshold, or are manufactured using Chinese-origin rare earth technologies. This is a landmark escalation of jurisdictional reach.
- Scope covers: parts, components, assemblies, magnets — threatens global defense, automotive, semiconductor, AI data center supply chains.

**Truce provision:** China agreed to suspend the Wave 2 controls for one year as part of the November 2025 extended tariff truce. Controls apply only on a suspended basis; underlying controls remain on the books.

## 4. TECH / SEMICONDUCTOR

### DeepSeek AI

**January 2025 — The "DeepSeek Moment":**
- DeepSeek R1 model released; surpassed ChatGPT to become #1 on US Apple App Store on January 27, 2025.
- Claimed training cost: ~$6 million (vs. ~$100 million for GPT-4). Benchmarked comparably to OpenAI o1.
- Sent shockwaves through Western AI investment thesis.

**2025 Model Progression:**
- V3.1 (MIT License): August 21, 2025
- V3.1-Terminus update: September 22, 2025
- V3.2-Exp: September 29, 2025
- DeepSeek prepped AI Agent for end-2025 release to rival OpenAI agents.

**Scale impact:**
- Chinese open-source models now power ~30% of global AI usage (up from 1.2% in late 2024).
- Founder Liang Wenfeng published architectural paper in early 2026 proposing fundamental rethink of foundational model training.

**2026 pipeline:**
- V4 model (coding-optimized) in testing; reportedly outperforms Claude 3.5 Sonnet and GPT-4o on coding benchmarks.
- Chinese labs shifting to domestic GPU alternatives from Huawei and Chinese GPU startups.

### Semiconductor Self-Reliance

**Huawei:**
- 2025 production target: ~600,000 Ascend series AI chips; aiming for 1.6 million dies by 2026.
- Ascend 950 family rollout in Q1 2026.
- Kirin 9030 processor (Mate 80 Pro Max) produced using evolved SMIC technology.

**SMIC:**
- Relies on ASML DUV (deep ultraviolet) lithography for 7nm and limited 5nm output.
- Expanding with non-US equipment (Chinese + Japanese tools).
- By end-2025: Chinese foundries expected to hold >25% of global top-10 mature-node capacity, mostly 28/22nm nodes.
- 5nm capability has poor unit economics without EUV; no clear path beyond 5nm.
- Total advanced capacity forecast by end-2026: ~100,000 wafers across Hua Hong, Huawei fabs.

**Critical limitation:** China remains a decade behind at the leading edge (3nm and below); the investment gap is massive but incremental progress is real and accelerating.

### US Chip Export Control Gyrations 2025

| Date | Action |
|---|---|
| Early 2025 | Biden-era H20/H800 restrictions in place |
| April 2025 | Trump administration tightened restrictions further; banned H20 exports to China; Nvidia took $5.5 billion hit |
| Mid-2025 | Trump reversed; approved H20 and AMD MI308 license applications |
| July 2025 | Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang declared export controls "not achieving their goals" |
| December 8, 2025 | Trump announced conditional H200 exports to China permitted with 25% fee; BIS reviews H200, AMD MI325X on case-by-case with security requirements |

**China's counter-moves:**
- China's Cyberspace Administration **barred domestic tech firms (ByteDance, Alibaba, etc.) from purchasing Nvidia AI chips** — directed them to use domestic suppliers instead.
- Firms ordered to stop testing and ordering Nvidia RTX Pro 6000D server.
- This creates a floor of guaranteed demand for Huawei Ascend and domestic alternatives.

## 5. GEOPOLITICAL

### China–Russia Relations

**High-level meetings (2025):**
- Putin visited China August 31–September 3 (4-day state visit; SCO summit + Victory Day Parade in China).
- Meetings also in Moscow (May) and Beijing (September).

**Economic ties:**
- Bilateral trade above $200 billion for three consecutive years; minor decline in 2025 described as "indicator adjustment."
- Agricultural trade +20%+.
- 20+ cooperation agreements signed (energy, aerospace, AI, agriculture, health, education, media).

**Strategic trajectory:**
- Xi launched **Global Governance Initiative** at SCO to promote Chinese leadership and challenge US-led multilateral institutions.
- China initiated trial visa-free travel for Russian citizens: September 15, 2025 – September 14, 2026.
- 2026–2027 designated China-Russia Years of Education.
- 2026 marks 30th anniversary of strategic partnership.

**Military dimension:**
- February 2026 reports: China expanding aid for Russia's war — drones, minerals, dual-use goods.
- China maintains deniability but Western officials say material support is intensifying.

**Assessment:** The partnership is deepening despite tactical friction. China sees Russia as a hedge against US pressure and a test case for challenging the Western-led order.

### China Response to US Strikes on Iran (Early 2026)

**Context:** US and Israel struck Iran, killing Iran's supreme leader (timing approximately late February/early March 2026 based on press conference dates and CNN analysis published March 4, 2026).

**China's official response:**
- Declared strikes have no UN Security Council authorization; violate international law.
- "Firmly opposed and strongly condemned" the attack.
- Called for parties to stop military operations.
- Filed formal protests; joined Russia in raising diplomatic voices at UN.

**Actual leverage:** Minimal. China's response was "predictably restrained." Beijing cannot materially deter US-Israeli military action once set in motion.

**Strategic framing:** China is using the Iran strikes to reinforce messaging to the Global South that Washington acts as a hegemonic power, while Beijing presents itself as a champion of non-interference.

**Cold calculation:** For Xi, the US-China relationship (trade, Taiwan, economy) vastly outweighs the relationship with Iran. The Iran issue is unlikely to upend US-China ties; Xi reportedly eyes a summit with Trump in Beijing.

**Taiwan implication:** Analysts note China is watching closely how the international community accepts US military action. Carnegie: "Watching the international community accept US actions will almost certainly convince Beijing that a military move against Taiwan... would be far more justifiable."

### China Response to US Venezuela Intervention (December 2025 – January 2026)

**Context:** US military operation against Venezuela's Maduro government (late December 2025).

**China's economic exposure:**
- ~$67 billion invested in Venezuela since 2007, primarily energy.
- ~90% of Venezuelan oil exports flowing to China by late 2025.
- China is Venezuela's largest creditor.

**China's response:**
- Sharp diplomatic condemnation: accused US of "hegemonism," "unilateral bullying," violation of sovereignty.
- Foreign Minister Wang Yi phone call (December 17) expressed support for Venezuela's right to defend sovereignty.
- **But offered nothing beyond rhetoric** — no material assistance, no military support, no threat of retaliation.

**Strategic impact:**
- China's Latin American strategy was premised on economic engagement being protected by the international legal order; Venezuela demonstrates that premise was contingent on US restraint.
- US intervention has increased geopolitical risk premium on all Chinese investments in Latin America.
- Carnegie: "The US Venezuela Operation Will Harden China's Security Calculation" — particularly regarding Taiwan. Beijing interprets acceptance of US action in Venezuela as a green light for broader use of hard power.

### Taiwan Strait Situation

**2025 Escalation Pattern:**
- Near-daily PLA aircraft incursions into Taiwan's ADIZ.
- PRC coast guard began patrols into Pratas waters (February 2025); ISW confirmed 32 incursions in 2025.
- Kinmen incursions: 46 confirmed in 2025.
- PRC is systematically testing and eroding the informally respected contiguous zone (12-nautical-mile buffer) around Taiwan's territorial waters.

**"Justice Mission 2025" Exercise (December 29–30, 2025):**
- Large-scale blockade rehearsal around Taiwan.
- 130 PLA aircraft sorties directed toward Taiwan; 90 crossed the Taiwan Strait centerline.
- Focused on: sea/air combat readiness patrols, blockading key ports, three-dimensional external-line deterrence.
- Second major exercise of its kind in 2025.
- G7 foreign ministers expressed "deep concern."

**2027 Window:**
- Xi's declared deadline for PLA readiness to invade Taiwan remains 2027.
- The purge of Zhang Youxia and Liu Zhenli — the last senior commanders with real combat experience — has raised serious questions about actual readiness to meet that deadline.
- Two interpretations remain contested: (a) Xi is prioritizing loyalty over competence as a preparatory step; (b) Xi's paranoia about a military coup has overridden strategic rationality.

**Taiwan's response:**
- Taiwan raised defense spending to 3.5% of GDP (NATO minimum) for core defense in 2025.
- Additional 1.5% of GDP earmarked for infrastructure.

### Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) 2025

- **Total 2025 BRI engagement: $213.5 billion** (construction contracts + investments; ~350 deals)
- **+19% increase in deal count** vs. 2024.
- H1 2025: highest six-month BRI engagement ever ($123 billion).
- **Energy dominated:** Oil ($42 billion), gas ($27 billion) — fossil fuels >74% of overseas energy engagement.
- **Green energy record:** $9.7 billion (wind + solar).
- **Top regions:** Africa, Central Asia, Middle East (>two-thirds of investment).
- Notable: Nigeria's Ogidigben industrial park alone = $20 billion.
- **Latin America severely underweighted:** 1.14% of construction, 0.4% of investment in H1 2025 (partly impacted by Venezuela disruption).
- **Membership:** 146–150 states as of May 2025.

## 6. POLITICAL

### Fourth Plenum (October 2025)

**Attendance signal:** Only 168 of 205 full Central Committee members attended — an "unprecedented proportion" had been purged or fallen into political trouble since the Committee was formed in 2022.

**Key decisions:**
- Solidified Xi's "core position" formally in communiqué.
- Adopted recommendations for the **15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030)**: emphasis on "self-reliance and strength in science and technology," "steering development of new quality productive forces," resilient supply chains capable of weathering "dangerous storms."
- Marked decisive shift: away from headline GDP maximization, toward innovation-driven and security-anchored growth model.
- **Personnel:** Elevated Zhang Shengmin to CMC Vice Chairman (replacing He Weidong); only general officer promoted.
- **Military purge formalized:** October 2025 plenum announced removal of multiple CMC members including Miao Hua, Lin Xiangyang.

**Assessment:** Some observers (Vision Times) described the Fourth Plenum as signaling "Xi's weakened grip as military and party reassert control"; official Chinese sources framed it as Xi consolidating authority. The attendance data supports underlying elite instability.

### Two Sessions 2026 (March 4–12, 2026)

**Opening session:** March 5, 2026 (NPC); CPPCC opened March 4. Session runs through March 12.

**Key announcements:**
- **GDP target 2026:** Around 4.5%–5% growth
- **Defense budget:** 1.91 trillion yuan ($277 billion); +7% YoY; 11th consecutive year of single-digit growth
- **Fiscal deficit:** Maintained at 4% of GDP
- **15th Five-Year Plan (2026–2030) officially presented** to legislature:
  - Eight priority areas from December 2025 CEWC: expanding domestic demand, fostering innovation, deepening reform/opening, balanced regional development, green transition, social welfare, preventing key-sector risks
  - Technology self-reliance as overarching theme
- China's military delegation notably missing a deputy leader at the session (Vision Times, March 8, 2026) — purge continues.

### Two Sessions 2025 (March 4–11, 2025)

**Key announcements:**
- GDP target: 5% (met exactly)
- Defense budget: +7%
- Fiscal deficit: 4% (historic)
- Ultra-long-term special treasury bonds: 1.3 trillion yuan
- **Private Economy Promotion Law** passed — legally codified private sector protections for first time
- Monetary policy shift to "moderately loose" formalized
- Foreign Minister Wang Yi characterized certainty as "a scarce resource" — positioned China as global stability anchor

### "New Quality Productive Forces" — Implementation Status

**Concept origin:** Xi Jinping introduced the phrase in late 2023; formalized at 2024 and 2025 sessions.

**Definition:** Innovation-led growth replacing traditional land/labor/capital input model. Centered on: emerging technologies, smart manufacturing, digital transformation, strategic emerging industries.

**2025 targets (set 2024; implementation review in 2025):**
- Breakthroughs in 100 cutting-edge technologies ✓ (ongoing)
- 100 "iconic products" — examples: humanoid robots, quantum computers, 6G networks, brain-computer interfaces, new displays, third-generation internet
- 100 leading enterprises
- 100 professional service institutions

**Strategic focus sectors for 2025–2026:**
- Commercial aerospace and low-altitude economy (drone economy)
- Biomedicine
- Quantum technology
- AI and embodied AI (robotics)
- 6G
- Biomanufacturing

**State Council Report (June 2025):** Documented implementation progress; acknowledged uneven results across regions, particularly in Western China.

**15th Five-Year Plan framing:** New quality productive forces are explicitly enshrined as the engine of China's 2026–2030 development model. The concept has migrated from political slogan to the formal planning architecture.

## SUMMARY: KEY IMPLICATIONS FOR BEHAVIOR MATRICES

### Xi Jinping Risk/Threat Calibration (Updated)

| Factor | Prior State | Current State (Mar 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Military loyalty | High (trusted Zhang Youxia) | SEVERE UNCERTAINTY — CMC gutted, only loyalist Zhang Shengmin remains |
| Taiwan timeline pressure | 2027 as soft target | 2027 as hardening deadline; purge may signal acceleration OR delay |
| Economic vulnerability | High (property, deflation) | Persistent — property not bottomed; deflation structural; youth unemployment endemic |
| Trade war management | Escalatory/defensive | Managed: tariff truce holds to Nov 2026; diversification accelerating |
| Domestic legitimacy | Nationalism-technology narrative | Reinforced by DeepSeek; "self-reliance" now dominant ideology |
| Geopolitical isolation | Moderate | Decreasing: BRI record, ASEAN FTA upgrade, Canada deal, Russia partnership deeping |
| Succession/elite stability | Uncertain | Significantly degraded — CCP CC attendance at Fourth Plenum lowest since Cultural Revolution era |
| AI/tech confidence | DeepSeek shock (positive) | Very high — domestic AI narrative is a key legitimacy resource |
| Response to US hard power | Venezuela + Iran: verbal only | Revealed preference: China will not intervene militarily to defend third-party partners; will use episodes rhetorically |

### Key Behavioral Signals for Simulation

1. **Military instability is not the same as Xi weakness** — Xi is actively constructing a more personal command structure. The simulation should model reduced PLA institutional agency with increased Xi direct-command probability.
2. **Economic stimulus has a structural ceiling** — Xi will not tolerate large-scale consumption stimulus (redistribution); the model should keep the "social insurance spending" lever constrained.
3. **Taiwan 2027 pressure is real but readiness is degraded** — model a bimodal distribution: either Xi accelerates because the political window requires it, or he defers because the professional military is too disrupted.
4. **Trade war: Xi has proven willing to negotiate** — the tariff truce reveals Xi's bottom: he will deal at acceptable terms rather than pursue economic autarky at full cost.
5. **Rare earth controls as a persistent leverage instrument** — these are on the books and can be re-activated; model as a latent escalation lever that activates when tariff pressure exceeds threshold.
6. **US military actions in Iran/Venezuela have recalibrated Xi's Taiwan calculus** — the simulation's geopolitical contagion model should register the international community's acceptance of US hard power as a potential accelerant for Xi's Taiwan decision timing.
