## 1. TARIFFS / TRADE WAR

### Background: "Liberation Day" (April 2, 2025)
- **April 2, 2025 — "Liberation Day"**: Trump declared national emergency over trade deficit, announced "reciprocal tariffs" on virtually all US imports
  - Universal baseline: **10%** on all imports (effective April 5)
  - Higher country-specific rates: EU 20%, China 34% (on top of existing 20% = **54% total**), Vietnam 46%
  - Announced April 9 go-live for elevated rates
- **April 9, 2025 — Market Crash + 90-day pause**: S&P 500 lost ~10% in 2 days ($6.6 trillion market cap wiped — largest 2-day loss in history). Trump paused all elevated reciprocal tariffs (above 10%) for 90 days for all countries EXCEPT China
- **China escalation**: US-China tariff spiral reached US tariffs of **145%** on Chinese goods, Chinese tariffs of **125%** on US goods

### China Trade War Resolution (May 2025 – November 2025)
- **May 12, 2025 — Geneva Truce**: US-China "Joint Statement on Economic and Trade Meeting in Geneva"
  - Both sides reduced April tariffs from 125% down to **10%** each for 90-day truce
  - Baseline pre-April tariffs (fentanyl, Section 301) remained in place
- **August 12, 2025 — Extension**: Truce extended another 90 days
- **November 2025 — One-Year Deal**:
  - Extended tariff reduction through **November 10, 2026**
  - US removed 10 additional percentage points from fentanyl tariffs
  - China halted fentanyl precursor flows
  - China eliminated export controls on rare earth elements / critical minerals
  - China agreed to purchase **25 million metric tons** of US soybeans/year in 2026-2028
  - Fortune headline: "Trump's 'done deal' with China just brings tariffs back to Liberation Day levels"

### Trade Deals with Allies (May–July 2025)
- **May 8, 2025 — UK "Economic Prosperity Deal"**:
  - Duty-free access for UK aerospace exports to US
  - 100,000 vehicle quota at 10% tariff rate
  - UK reciprocated: duty-free quotas for US beef and ethanol
- **May 23 / July 27, 2025 — EU negotiations**:
  - Trump threatened 50% tariff on EU (announced May 23)
  - Delayed to July 9, then further negotiations
  - Final deal: **15% reciprocal tariff** on EU (down from threatened 50%)
- **July 2, 2025 — Vietnam Deal**: 20% baseline tariff; 40% on transshipments
- **Steel/Aluminum**: Raised to **50%** globally in June 2025 (UK stayed at 25% during negotiations)
- **Japan/South Korea**: Trump sent letters July 7 threatening tariffs if no deal by end of July

### Supreme Court Ruling (February 20, 2026)
- **February 20, 2026**: Supreme Court ruled **6-3** that Trump lacked statutory authority under IEEPA to impose broad reciprocal tariffs
  - Case: *Learning Resources, Inc. v. Trump*
  - Struck down: Reciprocal tariffs AND 25% Canada/Mexico/China fentanyl tariffs
  - **$175+ billion** in collected IEEPA tariff revenue at risk of refund ($160B collected through ruling date)
- **Trump response**: Announced temporary **10% global tariff** under Trade Act of 1974 (different legal authority)
  - Treasury pivoted to Section 232 and Section 301 authorities
  - China 1-year deal unaffected (different legal basis)

### Tariff Policy Status as of March 2026
- IEEPA-based reciprocal tariffs struck down
- 10% global tariff reimposed under Trade Act 1974
- China: ~55% effective rate (pre-Liberation Day Section 301 + fentanyl tariffs, minus 10pp, + 10% new global)
- EU deal at 15% stands (negotiated, not IEEPA)
- UK deal stands
- Significant legal uncertainty ongoing

## 2. MILITARY / FOREIGN POLICY

### Operation Rough Rider — Yemen/Houthis (March–May 2025)
- **March 15, 2025**: Trump ordered "decisive and powerful military action" against Houthis citing piracy/terrorism against US vessels
- Deployed **2 naval carrier groups**; over **1,000 airstrikes** by end of April
- US losses: multiple Reaper drones, 2 advanced F/A-18 fighters lost at sea during carrier evasive maneuvers
- Cost: Congressional estimate **>$1 billion**
- **May 6, 2025 — Ceasefire**: Trump declared strikes over; Oman brokered US-Houthi ceasefire
  - Houthis agreed to halt attacks on US vessels only
  - Houthis continued attacks on other vessels and vowed ceasefire did NOT apply to Israel
- **Context**: Houthi attacks began October 2023 in solidarity with Gaza; after Gaza ceasefire (October 2025), Houthis halted all strikes
- **Market impact**: Limited; contained to Red Sea shipping disruption premiums

### Nigeria Strikes — Christmas Day (December 25, 2025)
- **December 25, 2025**: US struck 2 ISIS camps in northwest Nigeria (Sokoto state) with Tomahawk missiles
- Executed by US Africa Command (AFRICOM) in coordination with Nigerian government
- Trump framed as defense of Christians; conducted on Christmas Day as symbolic message
- Target: Islamic State-Sahel Province (ISSP) / Lakurawa — group with ties to ISIS active in Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger, Nigeria
- Nigerian Foreign Minister confirmed coordination but denied religious framing
- **Market impact**: Negligible; no oil infrastructure targeted; Nigeria not major oil disruption risk

### Operation Absolute Resolve — Venezuela (January 3, 2026)
- **January 3, 2026 ~2 AM local time**: US launched "air, land and sea" military attack on Venezuela
  - **~150 aircraft** involved
  - CIA team had been tracking Maduro movements for months using inside source
  - Trump approved operation before Christmas 2025
- **Outcome**: President Nicolás Maduro and wife Cilia Flores **captured**, transferred to New York
  - Charges: narco-terrorism, drug trafficking, weapons charges
  - At least **80 killed** (including civilians and military personnel per Venezuelan officials)
  - Cuba announced 32 Cuban military personnel killed ("in combat, at request of Venezuela")
- **Political aftermath**:
  - Venezuelan Supreme Tribunal appointed Delcy Rodríguez as interim president (January 5, 2026)
  - Trump declared US would "run Venezuela temporarily"
  - Regional alarm from Latin American governments
- **Oil market reaction**: **MUTED**
  - Brent slipped to ~$60/bbl (WTI below $58) — actually slightly bearish
  - Venezuela produces only ~800,000 bbl/day; global market oversupplied
  - Oil rose ~2% on Monday after initial dip; analysts called impact "limited"
- **Stock market**: Mostly shrugged off; defense stocks outperformed
  - BAE Systems +4.4%; Rheinmetall (Germany) +6.1%
  - Gold and silver rose (safe haven demand)
  - Overall market largely flat; analysts noted "positive response is fascinating"

### US-Israel Strike on Iran / Khamenei Killed (February 28, 2026)
- **February 28, 2026**: US and Israel launched coordinated large-scale attack on Iran
  - Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei **killed**
  - Also killed: Iranian Defense Minister, IRGC Commander, Secretary of Iranian Security Council
  - Iranian state media confirmed Khamenei "martyred"
- Trump statement: Hailed death of "evil" Khamenei; vowed strikes on Iran to persist "until peace secured"
- **March 1, 2026**: Trump vowed to "avenge" deaths of US service members; combat operations continue
- **Oil price impact — SEVERE**:
  - Pre-strike: Brent ~$70/bbl
  - By March 2: Brent surged **10-13%** to ~$80-82/bbl
  - By March 9: Brent at **$94/bbl** (highest since September 2023) — up ~50% from January
  - Key risk: Strait of Hormuz — ~20% of global oil supply; described as "unprecedented" disruption
  - Brent crude settled at **$94/bbl** on March 9; EIA forecast >$95 for next 2 months
- **Stock market impact**:
  - March 1: S&P 500 closed flat (+0.04%) after falling 1.2% intraday; Dow down 400 pts (had been -1,200 pts)
  - March 3: Dow tumbled, partially recovered
  - March 9: World shares tumbled as Iran war pushed crude above $110
  - **As of mid-March 2026: S&P 500 down ~28% YTD (2026)**
  - JPMorgan warned: S&P 500 at risk of 10% correction to ~6,270 if conflict persists
  - JPMorgan: oil could hit $120/bbl in prolonged conflict scenario
- **Other market effects**:
  - Gulf state airspace closed (UAE, Qatar, Kuwait) — thousands of flights grounded; Emirates Airlines significant losses
  - Gas pump prices in US: average **$3.54/gallon** (+21% in one month) by early March
  - Risk of global recession flagged if disruptions persist

### Russia-Ukraine (No Peace Deal as of March 2026)
- **January-April 2025**: Trump envoy Keith Kellogg aimed for deal within 100 days (April 29 deadline)
- **April 23, 2025**: US presented "final peace proposal" — Russia responded with massive missile/drone attack on Kyiv; 13 civilians killed
- **November 2025**: Steve Witkoff (US envoy) and Russian counterpart Kirill Dmitriev developed **28-point peace plan**
- **December 28, 2025**: Trump declared "closer than ever"; Zelensky said 20-point framework "95% agreed," security guarantees "100% agreed"
- **January 2026**: Further negotiations scheduled in Washington (US, Ukraine, European countries)
- **Status**: No deal signed; Russia repeatedly refused ceasefire; territory remains thorniest issue
- **UK/France**: Proposed "military hubs" in Ukraine as part of peace security guarantees
- **Market impact**: Uncertainty premium in European equities; grain/energy markets disrupted throughout

## 3. DOMESTIC ECONOMIC POLICY

### DOGE — Department of Government Efficiency
- Led by Elon Musk (departed by late November 2025)
- **Workforce cuts**: 317,000 federal employees eliminated by end of 2025 (271,000 through November)
  - Federal workforce down **~12%** — largest peacetime workforce cut on record
  - Fastest federal employment reduction since President Carter
- **Spending outcome**: **FAILED to cut total spending**
  - Federal spending in first 11 months 2025: **$7.6 trillion** — $248 billion HIGHER than same period 2024
  - Cuts failed because most federal spending is entitlement programs (Medicare, Social Security, Medicaid)
  - DOGE had "no noticeable effect on trajectory of spending"
- **Musk assessment** (year-end): "A little bit successful" — stopped "entirely wasteful" spending; "wouldn't personally want to do it again"
- Musk left DOGE position (reported ceased to exist as structured entity by late November 2025)
- **March 2026 note**: DOGE cuts to civilian agencies cited as hampering US government response to Iran war

### Government Shutdown (October 1 – November 12, 2025)
- Duration: **43 days** — longest government shutdown in US history
- Cause: Congress failed to pass FY2026 appropriations
  - Senate Democrats blocked Republican bill over refusal to extend ACA subsidies expiring November 2025
- **Impact**: ~900,000 federal workers furloughed; ~2 million working without pay
- Resolution: House passed revised Senate bill November 12; Trump signed same day
- **Market impact**: Moderate; added to fiscal uncertainty; deficit revealed at **$1.8 trillion** for FY2025

### Debt Ceiling
- **July 4, 2025**: Budget reconciliation law (One Big Beautiful Bill) raised debt limit by **$5 trillion** to **$41.1 trillion**
- Total outstanding debt as of September 3, 2025: **$37.4 trillion** ($30.1T public + $7.3T intragovernmental)

### "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" — Tax Legislation
- **Signed**: July 4, 2025 (House passed 218-214 on July 3; Senate 51-50 on July 1, VP Vance tie-break)
- Passed via budget reconciliation (simple majority, Republicans only)
- **Key provisions**:
  - Permanently extended Trump 2017 individual tax rates (were set to expire end of 2025)
  - SALT deduction cap raised from $10,000 to **$40,000** for earners under $500,000 (reverts to $10,000 after 5 years)
  - New deduction: up to $12,500 ($25,000 MFJ) for overtime pay (retroactive to Jan 1, 2025)
  - Large spending cuts (Medicaid, SNAP) and defense increases
- **Fiscal impact**: Estimated to add **~$3 trillion** to national debt over 10 years; cut ~$4.46 trillion in tax revenue

### Federal Reserve — Powell Situation
- Powell **NOT fired** as of March 2026
- Trump repeatedly attacked Powell publicly, called for rate cuts, said "I'd love to fire" Powell
- July 2025: Threats became more concrete; Trump attempted to have Fed Board wrest control from Powell
- Legal constraints: Supreme Court (1935 precedent) protects independent agency commissioners; Fed carveout protects Powell
- White House legal team building "for cause" case based on Fed HQ renovation ($2.5B) and alleged Congressional perjury
- **Fed actions 2025**:
  - Held rates at **4.25-4.5%** through first half 2025 (citing tariff inflation risk)
  - Three 25bp rate cuts in H2 2025; **Fed funds rate: 3.50-3.75%** at year-end 2025
- **10-Year Treasury yield**: Started 2025 above 4.5%; dropped to 4.045% after Liberation Day tariff announcement; ended 2025 at ~**4.16%**
- March 2026: Yields spiking on Iran war / oil inflation concerns

### Bitcoin Strategic Reserve
- **March 6, 2025**: Trump signed executive order establishing **Strategic Bitcoin Reserve** and US Digital Asset Stockpile
- Capitalized with forfeited BTC from criminal/civil proceedings
- US holds estimated **328,372 BTC** (as of February 2026) — largest known state holder globally
- Policy: Will not sell reserve BTC; Treasury/Commerce authorized to acquire more "budget-neutrally"
- **Broader crypto policy**:
  - March 3, 2025: Trump announced reserve would also include SOL, ADA, XRP, ETH (multi-asset "Crypto Capital of the World" vision)
  - OCC/FDIC: Banks no longer need advance permission for crypto activities
  - April 2025: DOJ disbanded National Cryptocurrency Enforcement Team
  - **July 4, 2025 (GENIUS Act)**: Signed stablecoin legislation; allows banks, nonbanks, credit unions to issue stablecoins

## 6. IMMIGRATION — ECONOMIC IMPACT OF MASS DEPORTATIONS

### Scale
- Mass deportation operations began January 2025; intensified throughout year
- Enhanced border security + interior enforcement: **$170 billion** allocated in 2025 budget
- Additional deportation cost: **$82.7 billion** additional over 10-year budget window

### Labor Market Effects (2025 data)
- **Agriculture**: Total employment dropped **155,000 workers** (March–July 2025) vs. +2.2% growth same period 2024

## 7. KEY SIMULATION PARAMETERS / BEHAVIOR MATRIX UPDATES

### Trump Tariff Behavior Model
- **Pattern**: Announce maximum tariff → market crash → negotiate down to moderate levels
- **Escalation trigger**: Retaliation from trading partner → tit-for-tat spiral
- **Resolution trigger**: Stock market decline >8% → pullback/deal
- **China exception**: More sustained pressure; resolved via extended truce, not elimination
- **Legal constraint (NEW Feb 2026)**: IEEPA tariff authority struck down; future tariffs limited to Section 232/301/Trade Act 1974

### Trump Military Behavior Model
- **Operations**: Houthis (March 2025), Nigeria/ISIS (December 2025), Venezuela (January 2026), Iran (February 2026)
- **Market sensitivity scale**:
  - Houthis/Nigeria: Minimal market reaction (small economies, no oil infrastructure)
  - Venezuela: Minimal (oversupplied market, low production)
  - **Iran: SEVERE** (Strait of Hormuz = 20% global oil; Khamenei assassination = regime existential threat)
- **Trigger pattern**: Escalation when adversary threatens US assets or when Trump seeks "deal"

### Market Reaction Parameters
- **Tariff shock**: S&P -10% over 2 days; DXY -2% instantly; Treasury yields initially drop (flight to safety)
- **Trade deal**: Rapid recovery rally; all-time highs achievable within ~2-3 months of deal
- **Iran-scale military event**: Oil +50% in 2 weeks; S&P -28% YTD within ~2 weeks of conflict; Strait of Hormuz risk = extreme tail risk
- **Venezuela-scale event**: No meaningful market reaction; market "shrugs it off"

### Inflation / Fed Parameters
- Tariffs added ~1-2% to inflation trajectory in 2025
- Fed held rates through H1 2025 (tariff uncertainty), then cut 3x in H2
- Iran war / oil shock in March 2026 = inflation surge risk; may force Fed to halt/reverse cuts
- Trump pressure on Powell: persistent but failed to fire; Powell term runs through 2026

### Fiscal Parameters
- FY2025 deficit: **$1.8 trillion**
- OBBBA adds ~$3 trillion over 10 years
- Total debt: ~$37.4 trillion (September 2025)
- DOGE: Cut workforce 12% but INCREASED total spending by $248B vs. prior year
